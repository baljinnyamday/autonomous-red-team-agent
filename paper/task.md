Background ReportBackground Report
The Background Report should describe the research you have completed during the early stages of the project and your plans for the remainder of the project.
There is no mark associated with this report, but it a required component.
The Background Report will be read by your academic supervisor and the second marker. After submission, you meet with the academic supervisor and second marker to discuss the contents of the report, your plans for the remainder of the internship, and your progress so far.  The academic supervisor can also give you feedback on your academic writing.
The feedback on your Background Report is valuable as it shows the standard and nature of academic writing required, and what you can emphasise in your final report.  The text of the Background Report itself can be used in the Final Report — it's therefore a very useful component of your internship, both as a means of checking progress and planning for the future, and putting material in place for your eventual final report.
Report Contents
Background Section
You should summarise the essential material related to the subject of the project, for example a review of relevant academic literature. This material can be incorporated into the project final report.
Referencing should use the Vancouver format.
Progress Section
You should also include a separate section on progress that describes: the activities and accomplishments of the project to date; any problems or obstacles that might have cropped up and how those problems or obstacles are being dealt with; and plans for the next phases of the project.
Section Headings
There are no mandated section headings for this report. The best format will vary from project to project.
A format that works well for many types of project is:
Title Page
Table of Contents
Chapter 1 - Introduction
Introduce the project and the problem. Provide some context for a reader who is not an expert in this area.
Chapter 2 - Literature Review
Describe the work that has already been completed that is relevant to the problem you are addressing.
Some projects may need an additional section covering notation, key theories or other such technical information.

Chapter 3 - Technical Achievements/Experimentation
If you have completed any early experiments or tested out different methods, you can briefly discuss them in this chapter.
This chapter will not be relevant for all projects and you might not have reached this stage yet.
Chapter 4 - Project Plan
Discuss the plan for the remainder of the project. Include a timeline.
It is useful to define what success in your project will look like. You might be able to compare your system against existing systems.
If your ethical review has identified that you will need to get Imperial ethical approval for your project (e.g. you are conducting research using medical data), please flag this here.

Appendices
Unlikely to be needed for this short document, but you can include supplementary information if necessary.
Note, there is no need to include an Abstract or an Acknowledgements page within this document.
Some reports may use different chapter titles. That is fine.
Report Format and Length
Please see the main course folder for instructions and a template which apply to both the Background Report and the Final Report.
The maximum length for the Background and Progress report is 20 pages, plus appendices (if necessary). That page count includes all pages other than the appendices, including title pages and references. A typical length for these reports is 10 to 20 pages.
Report Review
This report will be reviewed by your academic supervisor, and the second marker.
You are expected arrange a meeting with your second marker after you submit the report, to receive verbal feedback.
